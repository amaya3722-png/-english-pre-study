Tell me about a time you turned around a struggling product line.

智能终端产品线转型 · S + T 环节
📚 自学 15 min 🎤 口语课 25 min 📅 Day 3

📋 Model Answer · B2 级理想回答

📋 "Tell me about a time you turned around a struggling product line."

When I took over our smart terminal product line in 2021, it was struggling. Here's the situation: We only had two models in the market, both running on the Qualcomm 8909 platform, which was already outdated. The designs looked old-fashioned, and we were losing ground to competitors.

My task was clear: Transform this product line from two aging products into a competitive, multi-platform portfolio.

I started by mapping out a three-generation product roadmap — from that single 8909 platform to a three-platform strategy covering Qualcomm, Unisoc, and MTK. This gave us a clear path forward instead of just reacting to the market.

📝 词汇卡片 · Chunk + 画面

不学单词，学 chunk。每个词的画面都是你的真实工作场景。

struggling product line
🔊 strug-ling（两个音节）
你接手时的那条产品线，像一艘漏水的船，就快沉了。
losing ground to competitors
🔊 ground 单数，不是 grounds
竞争对手的地盘在扩张，你的地盘在缩小——像地图上别人的颜色在吞噬你的。
outdated platform
🔊 out-DA-ted
一台 2015 年的安卓手机，怎么也跑不动新 App——这就是高通 8909。
three-generation product roadmap
🔊 road-map（两个词都重读）
办公桌上摊开的那张大表，Gen1→Gen2→Gen3，每个阶段标着技术指标和时间节点。
multi-platform portfolio
🔊 port-FO-lio
三座工厂（高通/展锐/MTK）同时运转，但产出的是统一的产品体系——就像三条生产线造同一款车。
transform into a competitive portfolio
🔊 trans-FORM
改造前：一辆破旧的自行车。改造后：三辆不同用途的跑车。

🎯 语法锚点 · 过去式一致性

今天整节 STAR 课的核心语法 = 所有 Action 用过去式。描述过去项目的每一句，找到动词 → 检查有没有 -ed 或不规则变形。

常见错误 vs 正确表达
❌ 我常犯✅ 正确表达
I take over the product line and start mapping out… I took over the product line and started mapping out…
We only have two models in the market We only had two models in the market
The platform is already outdated The platform was already outdated
描述过去 = 每个动词检查有没有 -ed 或是不规则变形。没变 = 错。

Let me tell you about a time I had to turn around a struggling product line. This was back in 2021, when I took over our smart terminal business.

Here is the situation. When I stepped into the role, we only had two models on the market. Both were running on the Qualcomm 8909 platform. Now, the 8909 was already outdated by that point. The processor was underpowered, the chip itself was expensive, and the industrial design looked old compared to what our competitors were putting out. We were losing ground. Our market share was shrinking, and honestly, the product line was heading toward irrelevance.

So my task was clear but ambitious. I needed to transform this product line completely. Not just refresh the existing models. Not just add one more platform. I needed to turn two aging products into a competitive, multi-platform portfolio that could serve different markets around the world. From a single outdated chipset to multiple modern platforms. From domestic only to truly global. That was the mission.

I knew the first thing I had to do was get the strategy right. So I mapped out a three-generation product roadmap. Generation one, stabilize what we had. Generation two, introduce the new platforms. Generation three, optimize for each target market. The roadmap moved us from that single Qualcomm 8909 platform to a three platform strategy covering Qualcomm, Unisoc, and MTK. This roadmap became the strategic backbone of everything that followed. It gave the whole team a clear direction, instead of just reacting to whatever the market threw at us.
